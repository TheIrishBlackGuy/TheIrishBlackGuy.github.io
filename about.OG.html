About me

Lyndon Livingston is a student at San Jose State University, majoring in Digital Media Art, who is trying to pursue a career as a type of artist. As a fan of video games such MineCraft, the Horizon series and retro gaming, he would enjoy the path pointing more so towards them. He aims to develop his skills in digital drawing, modeling, and coding. As he also has experiences filming, editing and pencil-to-paper drawing, he has a large collection of tools at his disposal.

Excercise 2

This exercise introducd new mechanics with new tools and their own challenges. With this exercise, I wanted to definitely try to make something adjacent to a game. So I wanted a moveable character but then based on the my characters movement, it reminded me of an old an old flash game(Fireboy & Watergirl) which had two independently controlled characters. So I wanted to give that a shot.

I made two characters independent of each other, one control with 'WASD' while the other controlled through '↑←↓→'. They each have there own theme which can be toggled between. They also have their own 'TRON-like' tail that follows them(It is visible while on their opposite theme). You can also trigger a key word which is linked to each of their themes.

The 404 bot

This character is a result of a prompt of creating a display to be shown when a web browser cannot be located also known as a 404 Error page. When thinking of the idea of a '404 Error', my first thought went towards something that can be something familiar to people that can be broken through a technical error. A robot was an obvious choice to me and it fit one of my art style of utilizing simple shapes to build and design a character.
